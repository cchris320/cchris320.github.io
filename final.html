What is SDG 3

13 Targets for a Healthier World

SDG 3 covers the full spectrum of health — from child mortality and infectious disease to mental health and universal healthcare access.

Target 3.1

Maternal Mortality

Reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to fewer than 70 per 100,000 live births by 2030.

Target 3.2

Child Mortality

End preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5 years of age.

Target 3.3

Infectious Diseases

End AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria and neglected tropical diseases; combat hepatitis and waterborne diseases.

Target 3.4

Non-Communicable Diseases

Reduce premature mortality from NCDs by one third through prevention and treatment by 2030.

Target 3.5

Mental Health

Strengthen the prevention and treatment of substance abuse, including narcotic drug and alcohol use.

Target 3.8

Universal Health Coverage

Achieve universal health coverage — quality essential services, medicines and vaccines for all.

Key Challenges

Health Crises We Must Address

Despite global progress, billions still face avoidable illness, death, and inequality in healthcare access.

🦟

Infectious Disease

Malaria, HIV/AIDS and tuberculosis continue to devastate communities in low-income countries with limited healthcare infrastructure.

608K malaria deaths in 2022 (WHO)
🧠

Mental Health Crisis

1 in 8 people worldwide lives with a mental disorder, yet global mental health spending is less than 2% of health budgets.

1 in 8 people affected globally
🏥

Healthcare Inequality

Wealthy nations have 10× more doctors per person than low-income nations. Where you are born determines your access to care.

10× doctor gap between rich and poor nations

What Can We Do

Every Action Counts

Achieving SDG 3 requires action at every level — from global policy to individual choices.

1

Stay Vaccinated

Vaccines prevent up to 3 million deaths annually. Keep your vaccinations up to date and encourage others to do the same.

2

Support Mental Health

Check in on those around you. Reduce stigma around mental illness by talking openly and seeking help when needed.

3

Advocate for Universal Coverage

Support policies that expand healthcare access. Write to your representatives. Vote for leaders who prioritize public health.

4

Donate & Volunteer

Organizations like MSF, WHO, and UNICEF save lives daily. Even small contributions fund medicines, vaccines, and training.

5

Adopt Healthy Habits

Exercise, nutrition, sleep, and avoiding tobacco reduce chronic disease burden — for you and for health systems worldwide.

6

Spread Awareness

Share accurate health information. Counter misinformation. Educating one person can change a community's health outcomes.
